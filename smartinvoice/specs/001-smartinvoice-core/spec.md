# Feature Specification: SmartInvoice Core

**Feature Branch**: `001-smartinvoice-core`
**Created**: 2026-04-21
**Status**: Draft
**Input**: User description: "Build SmartInvoice with 3 use cases: (1) Invoice Management — create,
track, auto-calc 18% GST, sequential INV-XXXX numbering, lifecycle draft→pending→paid/overdue,
persisted in MongoDB. (2) Dashboard — real-time stats, monthly revenue chart, recent invoices
sourced from MongoDB aggregations. (3) AI Insights — client payment scoring, risk classification,
3-month revenue forecast driven by MongoDB aggregation pipelines."

## Clarifications

### Session 2026-04-21

- Q: How does the business owner authenticate? → A: Username + password with session-based auth (no external dependency).
- Q: How is an invoice "submitted to the client"? → A: A "Mark as Sent" button changes status to pending; no email or PDF is generated by the system.
- Q: Can invoices be edited after creation? → A: Draft invoices are fully editable; pending, paid, and overdue invoices are locked (read-only).
- Q: How does the dashboard refresh its statistics? → A: Stats are accurate on page load and after a manual browser refresh; no background polling or WebSocket.
- Q: What is the unique identifier for a Client? → A: Email address is the unique key; the system rejects duplicate client email addresses.
- Q: Can invoice data be exported out of the system? → A: CSV export of the invoice list only (no PDF or JSON); intended for import into Tally / Excel / GST-filing workflows.
- Q: What is the deletion / retention policy? → A: Drafts can be hard-deleted; pending, paid, and overdue invoices cannot be deleted at all (preserved permanently for compliance).
- Q: Are partial payments supported? → A: No — atomic "Mark as Paid" only; an invoice is either unpaid or fully paid. Partial payments are out of scope for v1.
- Q: What are the password and session requirements? → A: Minimum 8 characters with at least one letter and one digit; session 24 hours absolute with 30-minute idle timeout.
- Q: How are numbers and dates formatted in the UI? → A: Indian convention — dates DD/MM/YYYY; monetary amounts grouped by lakh/crore (e.g., ₹1,23,456.78).

### Session 2026-04-22

- Q: Should per-invoice PDF download be supported? → A: Yes — the owner may download a single invoice as a GST-Rule-46-compliant PDF. Auto-email, auto-send on status change, and bulk PDF export remain out of scope. This extends but does not contradict Q2 and Q6 (which governed *automatic* delivery and *list* export respectively).
- Q: What are the semantics of recording a payment? → A: Atomic audit record — exactly one payment per invoice, amount MUST equal the invoice grand total. Optional method (upi / bank_transfer / cash / cheque / other) and reference fields are captured for audit. Partial payments remain out of scope (consistent with Q8).
- Q: How is the owner account provisioned? → A: First-run setup wizard — when no owner exists, the login screen shows a one-time setup form for username + password. After the owner is created, the wizard never appears again. No env-var secrets required.
- Q: Should HSN/SAC codes be captured per line item? → A: Optional per line item — the field is offered in the create form and stored when provided, rendered on the PDF when set, blank when omitted. Keeps small suppliers compliant without forcing the field on everyone.
- Q: What is the login throttling / lockout policy? → A: After 5 failed login attempts within 10 minutes, the account is locked for 15 minutes. Successful login resets the failure counter. No permanent lockout; the timer expires automatically.

## User Scenarios & Testing *(mandatory)*

### User Story 1 — Invoice Management (Priority: P1)

A business owner creates a new invoice for a client, enters line items with amounts, and the
system automatically calculates the 18% GST and assigns a sequential invoice number (INV-0001,
INV-0002, …). The invoice starts as a draft, is sent to the client (moving it to pending), and
is later marked paid or flagged overdue. The full invoice history is stored and retrievable at
any time.

**Why this priority**: Invoice creation and tracking is the core transactional workflow of
SmartInvoice. All other use cases depend on invoice data existing in the system.

**Independent Test**: Can be fully tested by creating an invoice with line items, verifying GST
auto-calculation and INV-XXXX number assignment, and advancing the status through the full
lifecycle — delivering a working invoice manager with no dependency on the dashboard or AI
features.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a logged-in business owner on the invoice creation screen,
   **When** they enter one or more line items with descriptions and amounts,
   **Then** the system calculates subtotal, 18% GST amount, and grand total automatically and
   assigns the next sequential invoice number in the INV-XXXX format.

2. **Given** a draft invoice,
   **When** the owner clicks "Mark as Sent",
   **Then** the invoice status transitions to pending and a submission timestamp is recorded.
   The system does not send any email or generate a PDF; the owner handles client delivery
   externally.

3. **Given** a pending invoice that has passed its due date without payment,
   **When** the system checks invoice due dates,
   **Then** the invoice status is automatically flagged as overdue.

4. **Given** a pending or overdue invoice,
   **When** the owner marks it as paid,
   **Then** the invoice status transitions to paid and a payment timestamp is recorded.

5. **Given** any invoice at any status,
   **When** the owner views their invoice list,
   **Then** the invoice appears with its current status, sequential number, amounts, and dates.

6. **Given** a new invoice is being created,
   **When** two invoices are created concurrently,
   **Then** each receives a unique, strictly sequential INV-XXXX number with no duplicates.

7. **Given** a draft invoice,
   **When** the owner edits any field (line items, client, due date) and saves,
   **Then** the updated values are persisted and GST/totals are recalculated automatically.

8. **Given** a pending, paid, or overdue invoice,
   **When** the owner attempts to edit any field,
   **Then** the system prevents the edit and displays a message that the invoice is locked.

### User Story 2 — Dashboard (Priority: P2)

A business owner opens the dashboard and sees a summary of their financial position at a glance:
total revenue, outstanding amount, number of overdue invoices, and a month-by-month revenue chart
for the current year. A list of the most recent invoices is shown without needing to navigate to
the full invoice list.

**Why this priority**: The dashboard provides the business owner with situational awareness. It
depends on invoice data (P1) existing but delivers immediate standalone value once invoices are
present.

**Independent Test**: Can be fully tested by verifying that all four summary statistics and the
monthly chart render correctly against a known set of invoices, and that changing invoice statuses
is reflected in the dashboard without a page refresh.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a business owner on the dashboard,
   **When** the page loads,
   **Then** the system displays: total revenue (sum of all paid invoices), total outstanding
   (sum of pending + overdue invoices), count of overdue invoices, and total invoices issued
   — all sourced from the current invoice data.

2. **Given** a business owner on the dashboard,
   **When** the monthly revenue chart renders,
   **Then** it shows one bar or data point per calendar month for the current year, each
   representing the sum of payments received in that month, with zero shown for months with
   no payments.

3. **Given** a business owner on the dashboard,
   **When** the recent invoices section loads,
   **Then** the five most recently created or modified invoices appear with invoice number,
   client name, amount, and current status.

4. **Given** an invoice is updated (e.g., marked paid),
   **When** the business owner loads or manually refreshes the dashboard,
   **Then** the summary statistics and recent invoices list reflect the updated data.
   The dashboard does not auto-refresh; a page load is required to see changes.

### User Story 3 — AI Insights (Priority: P3)

A business owner views AI-generated insights about their clients and revenue outlook. For each
client, the system scores their payment behaviour (on-time vs. late history) and classifies them
as low, medium, or high payment risk. A 3-month revenue forecast is displayed based on patterns
in historical invoice and payment data.

**Why this priority**: AI Insights deliver the highest-value intelligence layer but require a
sufficient volume of invoice history (P1) to produce meaningful outputs. It is independently
demonstrable once invoice data exists.

**Independent Test**: Can be fully tested by seeding a known invoice dataset and verifying that
client risk scores, classifications, and the 3-month forecast match the expected outputs derived
from that dataset — with no dependency on the live dashboard UI.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a business owner on the AI Insights page,
   **When** the client payment scoring section loads,
   **Then** each client is shown with a numeric payment score (0–100) calculated from their
   ratio of on-time to late payments, average days-to-payment, and volume of invoices.

2. **Given** a client payment score is computed,
   **When** the risk classification is applied,
   **Then** the client is labelled Low Risk (score ≥ 70), Medium Risk (score 40–69), or
   High Risk (score < 40), and the label is displayed alongside the score.

3. **Given** at least 3 months of paid invoice history exists,
   **When** the revenue forecast section loads,
   **Then** the system displays a projected revenue figure for each of the next 3 calendar
   months, derived from historical payment trends.

4. **Given** fewer than 3 months of paid invoice history,
   **When** the revenue forecast section loads,
   **Then** the system displays a clear message explaining that insufficient history is
   available, rather than showing a zero or blank forecast.

5. **Given** a client has no invoice history,
   **When** the client appears in the insights list,
   **Then** the system shows "No data" rather than a score of zero, to distinguish new
   clients from genuinely poor payers.

### Edge Cases

- What if the owner tries to add a client with an email that already exists? The system MUST
  reject the creation and prompt the owner to use the existing client record instead.
- Can a locked (pending/paid/overdue) invoice be edited? No — the system prevents all edits
  once an invoice moves out of draft status. The owner must create a new invoice if correction
  is needed.
- What happens when an invoice number sequence has a gap (e.g., a draft is deleted before
  being submitted)? The sequence MUST continue from the last issued number; gaps are
  acceptable and MUST NOT be backfilled.
- Can a pending, paid, or overdue invoice be deleted? No — once an invoice leaves draft
  status it is permanently retained. Any attempt to delete MUST be rejected with a clear
  explanation that sent invoices cannot be deleted for audit/compliance reasons.
- How does the system handle GST calculation on amounts with sub-paisa precision? All monetary
  amounts MUST be rounded to two decimal places using standard commercial rounding (half-up).
- What if a client has only one invoice? Payment scoring MUST still produce a valid score, not
  an error.
- What if the dashboard is loaded before any invoices exist? All stats MUST display zero/empty
  states gracefully with a prompt to create the first invoice.
- What if the revenue forecast pipeline times out or returns an error? The AI Insights page
  MUST show a degraded-mode message rather than a blank or broken view.

## Requirements *(mandatory)*

### Functional Requirements

**Authentication**

- **FR-000**: System MUST authenticate the business owner with a username and password before
  granting access to any feature. Sessions MUST be maintained via a server-side session cookie.
  Unauthenticated requests MUST be redirected to the login screen.
- **FR-000c**: On first application startup (when no owner record exists), the login screen
  MUST present a one-time setup form collecting username and password (subject to FR-000a).
  After the owner is created, this setup form MUST be hidden permanently; subsequent visits
  to the login screen show only the normal login form. The setup endpoint MUST be rejected
  with `409 Conflict` once an owner exists.
- **FR-000a**: Passwords MUST be at least 8 characters long and contain at least one letter
  and one digit. Passwords MUST be stored using a modern one-way hash (e.g., bcrypt/argon2);
  plaintext storage is prohibited.
- **FR-000b**: Sessions MUST expire after 24 hours absolute from login, or after 30 minutes
  of inactivity, whichever comes first. Expired sessions MUST redirect the user to the login
  screen on the next request.
- **FR-000d**: The login endpoint MUST apply progressive throttling. After 5 failed attempts
  against the same account within a rolling 10-minute window, further login attempts for
  that account MUST be rejected with `429 Too Many Requests` for 15 minutes. A successful
  login MUST reset the failure counter. The lockout MUST expire automatically; no permanent
  lockout is permitted.

**Invoice Management**

- **FR-001**: System MUST auto-calculate 18% GST on every invoice's subtotal and display the
  GST amount and grand total separately.
- **FR-002**: System MUST assign a unique, sequential invoice number in the format INV-XXXX
  (zero-padded to 4 digits, e.g., INV-0001) to every new invoice at the point of creation.
- **FR-003**: Invoice number assignment MUST be collision-free under concurrent creation
  requests.
- **FR-004**: System MUST support the invoice lifecycle: draft → pending → paid, with an
  automatic transition to overdue when a pending invoice passes its due date unpaid. The paid
  transition is atomic — the full grand total is settled in a single action. Partial payments
  and intermediate "partially paid" states are out of scope.
- **FR-004b**: When recording a payment, the system MUST accept exactly one payment record
  per invoice, whose amount MUST equal the invoice's grand total. The payment record MAY
  include optional `method` (one of upi / bank_transfer / cash / cheque / other) and free-text
  `reference` (e.g., transaction ID, cheque number) fields for audit. Attempting to record a
  payment of a different amount, or a second payment on an already-paid invoice, MUST be
  rejected.
- **FR-004a**: Draft invoices MUST be fully editable (all fields including line items, client,
  due date). Invoices in pending, paid, or overdue status MUST be read-only; the owner MUST
  NOT be able to modify any field once an invoice has been marked as sent.
- **FR-005**: System MUST persist every invoice and all status transitions in MongoDB.
- **FR-005a**: System MUST enforce email address uniqueness across all client records. Attempting
  to create a client with an email already in use MUST be rejected with a clear error message.
- **FR-005b**: System MUST allow hard deletion of invoices in draft status only. Invoices in
  pending, paid, or overdue status MUST NOT be deletable by any means; attempting to delete
  such an invoice MUST be rejected. This preserves the audit trail required for GST compliance.
- **FR-006**: System MUST allow a business owner to view a paginated list of all invoices,
  filterable by status.
- **FR-006a**: System MUST allow the business owner to export the current invoice list to a
  CSV file containing all invoice fields (invoice number, client name, client email, subtotal,
  GST, grand total, status, issue date, due date, payment date). The export MUST respect the
  currently active status filter. Bulk PDF and JSON list exports are out of scope.
- **FR-006b**: System MUST allow the business owner to download any single invoice (regardless
  of status) as a GST-Rule-46-compliant PDF. The PDF MUST include all Rule 46 mandatory fields
  (supplier GSTIN, invoice number, recipient details, CGST 9% + SGST 9% split, grand total,
  amount in words, place of supply). The system MUST NOT auto-email or auto-send this PDF to
  the client; download is owner-initiated only.

**Dashboard**

- **FR-007**: System MUST display four real-time summary statistics: total revenue (paid),
  total outstanding (pending + overdue), overdue invoice count, and total invoices issued.
- **FR-008**: System MUST render a monthly revenue chart for the current calendar year,
  sourced from a MongoDB aggregation pipeline.
- **FR-009**: System MUST show the five most recent invoices on the dashboard, sourced from
  a MongoDB aggregation pipeline.

**AI Insights**

- **FR-010**: System MUST compute a payment score (0–100) per client based on payment
  timeliness, average days-to-payment, and invoice volume, using a MongoDB aggregation
  pipeline.
- **FR-011**: System MUST classify each client as Low Risk, Medium Risk, or High Risk based
  on their payment score.
- **FR-012**: System MUST generate a 3-month revenue forecast using historical paid invoice
  trends from MongoDB aggregation pipelines.
- **FR-013**: System MUST handle the insufficient-history case (fewer than 3 months of data)
  for the forecast gracefully with a descriptive message.
- **FR-014**: System MUST handle clients with no invoice history by displaying "No data"
  rather than a default score.
- **FR-015**: All dates in the UI MUST be formatted as DD/MM/YYYY (e.g., 21/04/2026). All
  monetary amounts MUST be grouped using the Indian numbering system (lakh/crore) with the
  rupee symbol prefix — e.g., ₹1,23,456.78. CSV exports MUST also use these conventions so
  that imports into Tally / Excel retain the expected Indian formatting.

### Key Entities *(include if feature involves data)*

- **Invoice**: Represents a single billing document. Key attributes: invoice number (INV-XXXX),
  client reference, line items (description, unit price, quantity), subtotal, GST amount (18%),
  grand total, status (draft / pending / paid / overdue), issue date, due date, payment date.

- **Client**: Represents a business client being billed. Key attributes: name, email address
  (unique — system rejects duplicates), phone number, billing address (Indian address format),
  GSTIN (optional). Email address is the system-wide unique identifier for clients.

- **LineItem**: Embedded within Invoice. Key attributes: description, quantity, unit price,
  line total, HSN/SAC code (optional — Harmonized System of Nomenclature for goods or
  Service Accounting Code for services; rendered on PDF when provided, blank when omitted).

- **PaymentInsight**: Derived (not persisted) — computed per client from invoice aggregations.
  Key attributes: client reference, payment score, risk classification.

- **RevenueForecast**: Derived (not persisted) — computed from historical payment aggregations.
  Key attributes: forecast month, projected revenue amount.

## Success Criteria *(mandatory)*

### Measurable Outcomes

- **SC-001**: A business owner can create a complete invoice with line items, see the GST
  calculated, and save it in under 2 minutes.
- **SC-002**: Invoice numbers are always unique; zero duplicate INV-XXXX numbers exist in
  the system regardless of concurrent usage.
- **SC-003**: The dashboard summary statistics are accurate as of the most recent page load or
  manual browser refresh. No background polling or real-time push is required; statistics
  reflect the current state of all invoices at the moment the page is loaded.
- **SC-004**: The monthly revenue chart covers all 12 months of the current year, showing
  zero for months with no revenue, with no missing months.
- **SC-005**: Client payment scores are computed for 100% of clients who have at least one
  invoice, with no clients in the scored list missing a risk classification.
- **SC-006**: The 3-month revenue forecast is available within 3 seconds of page load for
  datasets up to 10,000 invoices.
- **SC-007**: The system displays a clear, user-friendly message (not an error or blank view)
  in every edge case: no invoices, insufficient forecast history, new clients with no data.
- **SC-008**: 100% of user sessions expire automatically after 24 hours absolute or 30 minutes
  idle; no stored password is readable in plaintext at any layer (database, logs, backups);
  after 5 consecutive failed login attempts within 10 minutes, further attempts for that
  account are rejected for 15 minutes (validated by contract test).

## Assumptions

- The system is a single-owner (single-tenant) application per deployment; multi-tenancy and
  team accounts are out of scope for this version.
- Authentication uses username + password with a server-side session cookie; OAuth, magic links,
  and passwordless login are out of scope.
- GST rate is fixed at 18% (CGST 9% + SGST 9%) for all invoices; variable GST rates per
  line item are out of scope.
- Invoice due dates default to 30 days from the issue date if not specified by the owner.
- The overdue check runs on demand (e.g., on page load or API request) rather than as a
  scheduled background job, for simplicity in the initial version.
- Currency is Indian Rupees (INR) only; multi-currency support is out of scope.
- UI uses Indian localization conventions: DD/MM/YYYY date format and lakh/crore number
  grouping (₹1,23,456.78). User-configurable locale is out of scope.
- The AI payment scoring and forecasting algorithms operate entirely within MongoDB aggregation
  pipelines; no external ML model or API is required.
- The forecast algorithm uses a simple trend-based projection (e.g., weighted moving average
  of monthly paid totals); advanced ML models are out of scope.
- Client GSTIN validation format checking is in scope; real-time GSTIN verification against
  a government API is out of scope.
- The application targets desktop browsers; mobile-responsive design is desirable but not a
  blocking requirement for the initial version.
- Invoice submission to a client means the owner clicks "Mark as Sent"; the system does not
  send emails, generate PDFs, or create shareable links. Client delivery is handled externally
  by the owner.
- Invoice data can be exported to CSV for use in external accounting or GST-filing tools;
  bulk PDF and JSON exports are out of scope for v1. Per-invoice PDF download (GST Rule 46
  compliant) is in scope and is owner-initiated only.
- Payments are recorded atomically: an invoice is either unpaid or paid in full. Tracking
  of advances, installments, or partial settlements is out of scope for v1.
